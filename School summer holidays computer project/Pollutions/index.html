Type of Pollution Description
Air Pollution Release of harmful substances into the atmosphere, such as gases, particulates, and biological molecules, causing harm to living organisms and the environment.
Water Pollution Contamination of water bodies (e.g., lakes, rivers, oceans) by harmful substances, making it unfit for human consumption and damaging aquatic ecosystems.
Soil Pollution Introduction of harmful chemicals, pollutants, or contaminants into the soil, affecting plant growth, animal health, and overall ecosystem balance.
Noise Pollution Excessive or disturbing noise levels in the environment that can cause annoyance, interference with communication, and adverse health effects.
Light Pollution Excessive or misdirected artificial light in the environment, disrupting natural light cycles, causing glare, and adversely affecting wildlife and human health.
